About HIV

What is HIV?

HIV (Human Immunodeficiency Virus) is a virus that attacks the body’s immune system, specifically the CD4 cells (T cells), which help the body fight infections. If left untreated, HIV can lead to AIDS (Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome), the most severe stage of HIV infection.

Having HIV does not mean a person has AIDS. With proper treatment, people with HIV can live long and healthy lives.

How is HIV Transmitted?

  • Unprotected sexual contact (vaginal, anal, or oral) with someone who has HIV.
  • Sharing needles, syringes, or other drug injection equipment.
  • From mother to child during pregnancy, childbirth, or breastfeeding.
  • Blood transfusion with contaminated blood.

HIV is not spread through casual contact like hugging, shaking hands, sharing food, or using the same toilet.

How to Prevent HIV

  • Use protection (such as condoms) during sexual activity.
  • Get tested regularly, especially if at higher risk.
  • Consider taking PrEP (Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis) if you are at ongoing risk.
  • Avoid sharing needles or syringes.
  • Ensure any blood products you receive are tested and safe.
  • For HIV-positive mothers: Early diagnosis and proper treatment can reduce the chance of passing HIV to their baby.
